What is Pauper?

Pauper is an Online Sanctioned Magic the Gathering format. It was officially established in 2005 by Alex Ullman and his colleagues, and has been one of the more popular formats in the game. A pauper deck can consist of any common throughout the history of the game, provided that it is not on the banned list. The format has constantly evolved over the years, and is enjoyed by many player both online and in paper. I have been playing this format on and off for several years now, and I really enjoy the format.

The Banned list

  • Grapeshot
  • Empty the Warrens
  • Temporal Fissure
  • Cloud of Faeries
  • Peregrine Drake
  • Treasure Cruise
  • Frantic Search
  • Cloudpost
  • Craial Plating
  • Invigorate

The following cards have been banned from the pauper format. Cards like Grapeshot, Empty the Warrens, and Temporal Fissure allow for some rather powerful storm win conditions, that can kill as early as turn 1. Things such as Peregrine Drake, Frantic Search, and Cloud of Faeries have allowed for infinite mana combo's to be prevalent. Invigorate caused strategies like infect to become dominant and too fast for the format to hand. CloudPost warped the late game, decks, and treasure cruise caused blue decks to become format warping. Cranial Plating was banned at the inception of the format, for being a mistake in card design, and too powerful of a card. All of these cards have earned their place on the ban list.

Deck Lists

One cool aspect of Pauper is that there are a variety of strategies in the format. This section will cover the two most powerful archetypes in the format currently, and give a general outlook to it.

  • 4 Augur of Bolas

Sorcery

Lands

  • 9 Snow-Covered Island
  • 2 Snow-Covered Mountain
  • 4 Evolving Wilds

The Delver deck aims to use tempo to win most of its games. It uses early selection like ponder and preordain to find the cards it needs, along with things such as spellstutter to counter the opponent's spells. Eventually, it will resolve the card Ninja of the Deep Hours and take over the game from there.

My Experience

I have been playing the Pauper format for several years now. I enjoy the complexity of the format, as well as how affordable it is compared to other formats in the game. Having all the cards be of common rarity means that the games are much more grindy, and as a result the games go long. The deck that I am currently piloting is Inside Out Combo.
